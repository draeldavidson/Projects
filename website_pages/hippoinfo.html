HOW TO SURVIVE AN UP-CLOSE HIPPO ENCOUNTER?

  1. If a hippo opens its mouth to flash its teeth, flee immediately.
  2. If you’re in the water, move in the opposite direction of where you saw the hippos.
  3. If you’re on land, find cover.
  4. You can’t outrun a hippo in a straight line, so find a tree, rock or vehicle to get between you and the hippo.
  5. This will help slow their attack and perhaps help you get safely to shelter.
  6. Run in a zig-zag if you have to
  7. The mass of a hippo makes it difficult for them to change direction, so “faking them out” by quickly changing directions can work in your favor long enough to get to safety.

HIPPO FACTS

  • They are the third largest land mammal on earth, after the elephant and the rhinoceros.
  • Their closest relatives are whales, from which they diverged about 55 million years ago.
  • They are semi-aquatic, inhabiting rivers, lakes and mangrove swamps
  • They spend the day submerged in river mud and come out at dusk to graze on grass.
  • They eat over 100 pounds of vegetation a day.
  • They can kill a crocodile.

Pablo Escobar, the world’s most infamous columbian drug runner, imported hippos from Africa to live in his backyard. Some of them escaped, and they’re an invasive species in the Amazon! There are reportedly between 40 and 60 hippos presently roaming the jungle.
